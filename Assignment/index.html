Australian Bushfires

Visualisation of Australian Bushfires from 2019-2020

More than 10.3 million hectares (103,000 sq km) of land - an area the size of South Korea - have been razed by bushfires across the country in recent months, with the east coast particularly hard hit. Thanks to NASA, data was collected by the Aqua and Terra satellites detecting real time measures of the Brightness Temperature (K) and FRP (Fire Radiation Power) from September 1st 2019 to 30th January 2020. This visualisation aims to gain useful insights into the severity and frequency of fires and where they most prevalently occur.

We can see the distribution of fires across Australia and the relative Brightness Temperature and FRP (Fire Radiation Power) that was detected by the NASA satellites. We can see that the data ranges within a FRP of 1000 to 4000MW. Just 1000MW is enough to power a town of over 1 thousand homes!. It can be observed that a large proportion of the fires occurred along the New South Wales and Victorian Border. Thus lets take a closer look at these fires along the NSW-VIC coastline.

Investigating the Climate of Most Affected Areas

According to an article published by Australian real estate agency Domain the regions most affected by the bushfires include; Blue Mountains (NSW), Central Coast (NSW), East Gippsland (VIC), Alpine Region (VIC) and the Nullarbor region (WA). Thus we will now investigate the climate of towns within these regions in order to understand why they are so prone to devastating bushfires.

The weather dataset used to draw insights is dated up to 2015. From analysing the following line graph we observe that the wind speed and temperature of the towns observed varies between 15-30 degrees celsius and around 10-25km/h wind speeds.

Investigting NASA's Satellite Measurements

NASA's satellite Aqua and Terra were used to observe the Australian bushfire datset. Both satellites employ MODIS (Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer) in order to obtain the calculations within the dataset. From the following visualisations we observe that Brightness is detected in higher magnitudes by the Aqua satellite but FRP is detected at lower magnitudes and vice-versa.

Confidence of Satellite Measurements

Each of the measurements that are observed by the satellites are given a level of confidence which indicates the relative certainty that we can provide towards the measurements for Brightness and FRP (Fire Radiation Power) that was observed. In the following scatter plot we observe the distribution of Brightness and FRP of the fires across Australia, and only measurements with confidence of greater that 95 have been included in the plot.
