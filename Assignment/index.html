Visualisation of Australian Bushfires from 2019-2020

The summer of 2019-2020 saw some of the most devastating bushfires the country has ever seen, causing massive loss of wildlife, forest land, and has even to led human casualties. Such catastrophes have made it incredibly difficult for the Australian Fire Department to contain these fires. Thanks to NASA, data was collected by the Aqua and Terra satellites detecting real time measures of the fires Brightness Temperature and FRP (Fire Radiation Power) from September 1st 2019 to 30th January 2020. This visualisation aims to gain useful insights into the severity and frequency of fires and where they most prevalently occur.

We can see the distribution of fires across Australia and the relative Brightness Temperature and FRP (Fire Radiation Power) that was detected by the MODIS satellite. It can be observed that a large proportion of the fires occurred along the New South Wales and Victorian Border. Thus lets take a closer look at these fires along the NSW-VIc coastline.

Investigating the Climate of Most Affected Areas

According to an article published by Australian real estate agency Domain titled "Road to recovery: 67 Australian towns to visit to help after the bushfires", the regions most affected by the bushfires include; Blue Mountains (NSW), Central Coast (NSW), East Gippsland (VIC), Alpine Region (VIC) and the Nullarbor region (WA). Thus we will now investigate the climate of towns within these regions in order to draw insights into why they are so prone to devatating bushfires.

The weather dataset used to draw insights is dated up to 2015. From analysing the following line graph we observe that the wind speed and temperature of the towns observed varies between 15-30 degrees celsius and around 10-25km/h wind speeds.

MODIS Satellite Modes

NASA's satellite Aqua and Terra were used to observe the Australian bushfire datset. Both satellites employ MODIS (or Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer) in order to obtain the calculations within the dataset. From the following visualisations we observe that Brightness is detected in higher magnitudes by the Aqua satellite but FRP is detected at lower magnitudes and vice-versa.

MODIS Satellite Modes

Each of the measurements that are observed by the satellites are given a level of confidence which indicates the relative certainty that we can provide towards the measurements for Brightness and FRP (Fire Radiation Power that was observed. In the following scatter plot we observe the distribution of Brightness Temperature and FRP of the fires across Australia, and only measurements with confidence of greater that 95 have been included in the plot.

MODIS Satellite Modes

We observe that Brightness Temperature and FRP (Fire Radiation Power) have a quadratic relationship with a greater density of measurements being recorded by Aqua. It can also be observed that a greater density of data points are plotted in the months of December and January in comparison to November. We can see that the data ranges within a FRP of 1000 to 4000MW. Just 1000MW is enough to power a city of over 1 million people!
